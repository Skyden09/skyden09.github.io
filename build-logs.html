Log #01: PETG Thermal Expansion & Gear clearance Seizure Tolerance Calculation
1. Problem Statement

During initial high-RPM testing of a 3D printed planetary gear system, mechanical binding occurred after 45 seconds of continuous rotation. Thermal buildup from friction caused the PETG planetary gears to expand, reducing the mesh clearance to zero and seizing the assembly.

2. Failure Analysis & Recalculation

Measured the initial radial clearance at 0.15 mm. Calculated PETG thermal expansion coefficient ($\alpha \approx 60 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{K}^{-1}$) under friction-induced heating ($\Delta T \approx 35^\circ\text{C}$). Recalculated mesh tolerances in Fusion 360 to include a revised pitch diameter offset of 0.28 mm and added 45° chamfers to horizontal contact edges.

3. Resolution & Results

Re-printed the planet gears at 240°C nozzle temperature with a 0.28 mm clearance. Testing yielded zero binding during a 15-minute continuous run with zero measurable backlash.

Initial Clearance 0.15 mm (Bound)
Optimal Clearance 0.28 mm (Smooth)
Extrusion Temp 240°C PETG
Log #02: FEA Stress Analysis — Gyroid vs. Cubic Infill for UAV Mounts Material Optimization
1. Problem Statement

Standard motor mounting brackets printed with 20% grid infill experienced shear failure along layer boundaries during sudden motor acceleration spikes on test rigs.

2. Testing Methodology

Modeled the motor mount in SolidWorks and conducted finite element analysis (FEA) to locate high stress concentrations. Printed three test coupons using 20% Grid, 25% Gyroid, and 30% Cubic infill settings to evaluate isotropic strength along the Z-axis.

3. Results & Weight Savings

The 25% Gyroid infill pattern distributed torsional load evenly across three axes, preventing shear along layer boundaries while reducing overall filament weight by 22% compared to the 30% Cubic alternative.

Infill Pattern 25% Gyroid
Weight Reduction -22% Filament
Shear Load Limit +38% Strength
Log #03: Airflow Simulation & Trailing Edge Thickness on Wing Sections Aerodynamics & CFD
1. Problem Statement

Standard 0.4 mm 3D printer nozzles leave thick trailing edges on aerodynamic airfoils, creating unwanted vortex shedding and increasing parasitic drag on UAV wing models.

2. Design Iteration

Switched to a 0.25 mm brass nozzle to print fine trailing edge geometry down to 0.3 mm width. Ran 2D airflow simulations (CFD) to observe boundary layer detachment at various angles of attack (0° to 12°).

Nozzle Diameter 0.25 mm
Trailing Edge 0.3 mm Precision
CFD Pass Rate Validated @ 12° AoA
